    DEPARTMENTS

    Computer Engineering

    Computer Engineering Department offers a comprehensive curriculum focused on programming, software development, networking, and system design, preparing students for careers in the tech industry with hands-on experience in modern labs.

    Electronic & Communication Engineering

    The Electronics and Communication Engineering Department provides a strong foundation in electronics, communication systems, signal processing, and embedded systems. The curriculum is designed to equip students with practical skills and theoretical knowledge, preparing them for careers in industries like telecommunications, hardware design, and automation.

    Chemical Engineering

    The Chemical Engineering Department, offers a comprehensive program focused on chemical processes, engineering principles, and industrial applications. Students gain hands-on experience in areas like process design, thermodynamics, and material science, preparing them for careers in industries such as petrochemicals, pharmaceuticals, and environmental engineering.

    Electrical Engineering

    The Electrical Engineering Department, provides a strong curriculum covering power systems, electrical machines, control systems, and renewable energy technologies. With a focus on both theoretical concepts and practical applications, students are prepared for careers in power generation, automation, and electrical infrastructure development.

    Civil Engineering

    The Civil Engineering Department, provides a well-rounded education in structural engineering, construction management, geotechnical engineering, and environmental studies. Students are trained in both theory and fieldwork, preparing them for careers in infrastructure development, urban planning, and construction industries.

    Mechanical Engineering

    The Mechanical Engineering Department, offers a comprehensive program focusing on mechanics, thermodynamics, manufacturing, and design. The curriculum equips students with practical skills and theoretical knowledge needed for careers in industries like automotive, aerospace, manufacturing, and robotics.
